# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This update summarises progress analysing UK STATS19 road safety data to support the client’s objective of identifying where serious road harm is concentrated and where interventions may be most effective. The analysis combines the collisions, casualties and vehicles datasets and focuses on KSI (Killed or Seriously Injured) outcomes. Initial results show that recent increases in serious injuries should be interpreted cautiously, as changes in reporting methods may partly explain the trend. The next stage of the analysis will identify high-risk locations and road user groups to support targeted road-safety actions.

- Decision the client needs to make:
  The client needs to decide what practical actions local authorities can take in areas with high levels of KSI collisions, particularly where higher speed limits are associated with greater serious harm.

- Why it matters:
  Although the analysis identifies locations and speed limits linked to higher KSI risk, nationwide speed limit policy changes are not feasible, so local authorities must focus on interventions they can realistically implement.

- What would change depending on the evidence:
